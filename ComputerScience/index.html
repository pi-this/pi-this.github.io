The CLI

CLI stands for Command Line Interface, according to w3schools.com. It's a way of interacting with computers by typing text commands. In the 1960s, CLI was the primary means of using computers and was widely used by Unix and DOS systems in the 1970s and 1980s. Nowadays, most users prefer graphical user interfaces (GUI), but CLI remains useful for software developers and system administrators. CLI commands can perform various tasks, such as copying, moving, deleting, or creating files and folders, and installing software. The specific CLI commands depend on the operating system and can be learned by using the help command.

Digital Art

Digital art refers to artistic work or practice that uses digital technology as an essential part of the creative or presentation process. This includes using computers, tablets, and other electronic devices to create works of art. Digital art can encompass various forms, including digital painting, digital drawing, graphic design, 3D modeling, and more. Artists can use specialized software and hardware to create their pieces, offering a wide range of possibilities for creativity and expression. Digital art has become increasingly popular due to its accessibility and the endless opportunities it provides for artists to experiment and create unique visual experiences.

What is Computer Science?

Computer science, as defined by the Merriam-Webster Dictionary, focuses on computational theory and computer design. It encompasses a wide range of topics related to computers, such as computer programming, digital art, and computer hardware. Any scientific focus related to computers falls under the umbrella of computer science.
